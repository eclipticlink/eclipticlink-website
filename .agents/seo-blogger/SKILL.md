# SEO Blogger

## When to Apply

Use this skill when:
- Writing or drafting a new blog post
- Reviewing or improving an existing article
- Planning blog topics or a content calendar
- Writing titles, meta descriptions, or headings for blog content

---

## 1. Before You Write: Research First

### Match search intent
Identify whether the post is:
- **Informational** — "How to…", "What is…" → write a guide with steps or definitions
- **Commercial** — "Best…", "vs…" → write comparisons with clear recommendations
- **Transactional** — "Hire…", "Get a quote…" → write service/landing content, not a blog

Check the top 5 Google results for the target keyword to understand expected format and depth.

### Choose keywords
- **Primary keyword**: one specific phrase the post targets (e.g. "AI integration for CRMs")
- **Secondary keywords**: supporting angles and semantic variations (use naturally)
- **Long-tail focus**: 4+ word phrases have clearer intent and lower competition

Avoid targeting extremely broad terms ("custom software", "AI") in single blog posts — compete on specific, answerable questions instead.

---

## 2. Post Structure

Every post must have this structure:

```
Title (H1)
Introduction (hook + what the reader will learn)
  ├── H2 Section 1
  │     └── H3 subsection (if needed)
  ├── H2 Section 2
  ├── H2 Section 3
  │     └── ...
  ├── Conclusion (summary + clear CTA)
  └── FAQ (optional, for featured snippets)
```

### Heading rules
- **One H1** — the post title; include the primary keyword near the start
- **6–8 H2s** — main topic sections; at least one H2 must contain the primary keyword
- **H3s** — subsections under H2s only; do not skip heading levels

### Content length
- Target **1,500–2,500 words** for most topics
- Match or exceed the depth of top-ranking competitors — don't pad with filler
- A focused 1,200-word post beats a rambling 3,000-word one

---

## 3. On-Page SEO

### Title (H1)
- **50–60 characters** — longer titles get truncated in search results
- Primary keyword **near the start**
- Use power words: "complete", "practical", "step-by-step", "proven"
- Numbers improve CTR: "7 Ways to…", "How to X in 5 Steps"

### Meta description
- **150–160 characters** (120 for mobile)
- Include the primary keyword naturally
- Make a clear promise: what the reader gets, not a vague summary
- Write it like a two-sentence sales pitch for the article

### Keyword placement
Place the primary keyword in:
- [ ] The H1 title
- [ ] The first 100 words of the introduction
- [ ] At least one H2 heading
- [ ] The meta description

Do **not** repeat the exact phrase mechanically. Use semantic variations naturally throughout.

---

## 4. E-E-A-T Signals

Google rewards content that shows **Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness**.

- **Experience**: Mention real projects, client outcomes, or team knowledge. "We've integrated AI into over X CRM systems…"
- **Expertise**: Show technical depth without unnecessary jargon. Reference specific tools, frameworks, or approaches.
- **Authority**: Link to 3–5 credible external sources (research, official docs, reputable publications — published within 2 years).
- **Trust**: Be accurate, cite statistics, disclose limitations, and keep content up to date.

For EclipticLink posts: tie insights to real services or client work where possible. This builds authority in AI integration and custom software development niches.

---

## 5. Readability Standards

- **Sentences**: 15–20 words average; vary length for rhythm
- **Paragraphs**: 3–4 sentences maximum — short paragraphs are easier to scan on mobile
- **Voice**: Active over passive. "We built the integration" not "The integration was built by us."
- **Jargon**: Define any technical term on first use; don't assume the reader's background

Use these elements to break up text:
- Bullet lists for multiple items (3+)
- Numbered lists for processes and steps
- Bold for key terms or important takeaways
- Tables for comparisons
- Blockquotes for notable statistics or expert quotes

---

## 6. Internal & External Links

| Type | Target | Guidelines |
|------|--------|------------|
| Internal | EclipticLink service pages, related blog posts | 3–6 per post; use descriptive anchor text |
| External | Authoritative sources (official docs, research, Gartner, etc.) | 3–5 per post; recent (within 2 years); open in new tab |

**Never use**: "click here", "read more", or "this article" as anchor text. Describe what the linked page is about.

---

## 7. Writing the Introduction

The introduction must do three things within the first 150 words:
1. **Hook** — open with a surprising stat, a direct question, or a relatable pain point
2. **Establish relevance** — confirm this post answers exactly what the reader searched for
3. **Preview** — briefly tell the reader what they will learn or walk away with

Include the primary keyword naturally within the first 100 words.

---

## 8. Writing the Conclusion + CTA

- Summarize 3–5 key takeaways in plain language
- End with a **clear call to action** that links to a relevant EclipticLink service or contact page
- Example CTAs: "Ready to integrate AI into your workflow? [Get in touch with our team](/contact)." or "Explore our [custom software development services](/services/custom-software-development)."

---

## 9. FAQ Section (Optional but Valuable)

Add a FAQ section at the end for posts targeting "how to" or "what is" queries. FAQ content is eligible for Google FAQ rich results (more SERP visibility).

- Write 4–6 questions people actually search
- Answers: 40–80 words each — concise and direct
- Structure as `<dl>` (definition list) in the blog component, or as H3 + paragraph pairs

---

## 10. Pre-Publish Checklist

Run through this before publishing:

### Content
- [ ] Title is 50–60 characters with primary keyword near the start
- [ ] Meta description is 150–160 characters with keyword and a clear promise
- [ ] Introduction includes primary keyword within the first 100 words
- [ ] At least one H2 contains the primary keyword
- [ ] Content depth matches or exceeds top-ranking competitors
- [ ] Conclusion has a clear CTA linking to a service page or contact

### Structure
- [ ] One H1; 6–8 H2s; H3s only under H2s
- [ ] Short paragraphs (3–4 sentences max)
- [ ] Lists, bold, and tables used to aid scanning

### Links & Trust
- [ ] 3–6 internal links with descriptive anchor text
- [ ] 3–5 external authority sources cited (within 2 years)
- [ ] E-E-A-T signals present (experience, expertise, cited sources)

### Technical
- [ ] Images have descriptive alt text (include keyword if natural)
- [ ] Canonical URL is set correctly
- [ ] JSON-LD (BlogPosting or FAQPage) is added if applicable

Focus on long-tail, answerable questions in these clusters:

- "How to integrate AI into your existing CRM"
